# Product Capabilities

## Capability levels

Capabilities are described against clear maturity levels so commitments are not overstated:

- Generally available: shipped and available to enterprise customers today.
- Architecturally possible: supported only under specific deployment options (for example, HA) and addenda.
- Roadmap only: planned but not committed in an RFP response.
- Requires custom scoping: feasible only after technical scoping.
- Contractually approved: permitted only when covered by approved policy or addendum.

## Generally available

Core security monitoring, role-based access control, MFA for administrative access, SSO, encryption at rest and in transit, and a configurable retention model are generally available.

## Requires scoping or addendum

99.9% uptime, customer-managed encryption keys, and custom integrations require specific deployment options, scoping, or a separate addendum.

## Approved wording

We describe capabilities as generally available, available under specific deployment options, on the roadmap, or requiring scoping, so the RFP response reflects what is actually committed.

## Forbidden wording

Do not commit roadmap features as available, and do not present scoping-dependent capabilities as standard.
